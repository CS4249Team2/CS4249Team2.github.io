Emergency Medical Puppy Aid
(This appeal has closed)


​We would like to thank you for your very generous support of the puppies!

Tiny is doing very well after her blood transfusion at The Animal Doctors, Singapore! She will be ready to go to her foster home real soon. Maxi and Teeny are recuperating in their foster home now after lots of TLC from the staff of Animal World Veterinary Clinic. There is no way we can do what we do without the support of vet clinics and we thank them for not turning a blind eye to street dogs even in these tough times.

Last but not least, mega shout out to Neil, Suyin and Freda for fostering the puppies and taking care of them and all their medication feeds. Foster parents are truly indispensable in our rescue work.

We will close the medical appeal as we have raised sufficient funds to cover the sisters' medical care.

​ Thank you for loving street animals as much as we do ❤️


Background: Early May was a difficult week for us. Due to the wet weather, many of the street dogs without shelter were getting drenched out in the rain. Sadly, one of the street dogs in an area we actively work in had given birth a month ago. She is one of two females we have yet to trap for sterilisation while the rest of the pack is sterilised. She oddly left her puppies behind and has not been sighted, we are unsure what has happened to her. 

Aunty Lee Chew who feeds the dogs in the area alerted us that the puppies were ill and weak. 3 of whom passed away from hyporthermia and babesia in spite of efforts to help them. 3 puppies are surviving with 1 in a serious state. She has since received a blood transfusion from a very kind doggie donor belonging to Lisa. We would like to give a shout out to Lisa and Sindu for going all out to help us get a suitable blood donor. 
​Do note that  should funds raised exceed the final medical bill, the surplus will be utilised for animals under our community care program that need assistance. ​
